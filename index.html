About Rwanda

Rwanda, known as "The Land of a Thousand Hills," is a small, landlocked country in East Africa that has emerged as one of the continent's most remarkable success stories.

From the misty volcanoes of the north to the savanna plains of the east, Rwanda offers an incredible diversity of landscapes and wildlife experiences. The country is renowned for its conservation efforts, particularly the protection of endangered mountain gorillas.

Safety & Security

Rwanda is consistently ranked as one of the safest countries in Africa, with low crime rates and a stable government.

Climate

Tropical highland climate with temperatures averaging 20-25°C (68-77°F) year-round. Two rainy seasons: March-May and October-November.

Culture

Rich cultural heritage with traditional dance, music, and crafts. Known for hospitality and the famous "Agaciro" (dignity) spirit.
